Mechanisms for Network Security

Firewalls

Act as a barrier between a trusted internal network and untrusted external networks. There are various types of firewalls, distinguished by the network layer at which they perform filtering.

Virtual Private Networks

VPNs establish secure, encrypted communication channels over untrusted networks (such as the internet), ensuring that data transmitted between endpoints remains confidential and secure.

IDS and IPS

IDS monitors network traffic for suspicious activities or patterns, while IPS goes a step further by actively blocking or preventing malicious activities.

Access Control

Access control ensures that only authorized users or devices have access to specific network resources. It can be implemented with role-based AC, strong authentication mechanism and proper user account management.

Network Segmentation

Dividing a network into segments or subnetworks can limit the impact of a security breach and prevent lateral movement for attackers.

Patch Management

Regularly updating software, operating systems, and firmware helps close known vulnerabilities and protect against exploits. It is therefore important to have a process of applying patches, updates and security fixes.
